# Pitch Script & Storyboard Template

Structure for creating compelling pitch packages — elevator pitch, video storyboard, and key messaging. Designed to work with the stakeholder persona from Stage 3 for targeted rehearsal.

## Elevator Pitch Formula

**30 seconds / ~75 words.** Four beats:

```
[HOOK]: For [target user] who [pain point from Stage 1 research],

[SOLUTION]: [Product name] is a [category] that [primary benefit].

[PROOF]: In our research, [specific data point from debate] — 
         [what real users said that validates this].

[ASK]: We're looking for [what you need — funding, partnership, pilot customers, approval].
```

### Good vs. Bad Examples

**Bad** (generic): "We're building a smart fridge that uses AI to help families manage food waste."

**Good** (specific + evidence): "Families throw away $1,600 of food every year because they can't track what's in their fridge. FreshTrack monitors your inventory locally — no cloud, no cameras — and alerts you before things expire. Reddit users told us the #1 feature they'd need is recipe suggestions from expiring items. We're looking for 50 beta families to validate our notification timing."

## Storyboard Template

6-8 scenes, 30-60 seconds total. Each scene:

```markdown
### Scene [N]: [Title]
**Duration**: [X seconds]
**Visual**: [What the camera shows — be specific about framing, setting, actions]
**Audio**: [Dialogue, voiceover, or sound effects]
**Key message**: [The ONE thing the audience should understand from this scene]
**Transition**: [How it connects to the next scene]
```

### Recommended Scene Arc

1. **The Problem** (5-8s) — Show the pain. Real situation, real frustration. Don't tell — show.
2. **The Moment** (3-5s) — The "what if..." that sparked the idea. Quick transition.
3. **The Product** (8-12s) — Demo the core interaction. Show the Must-Have feature in action.
4. **The Magic** (5-8s) — The moment that makes viewers say "I want that." The feature that won the debate.
5. **The Proof** (5-8s) — Data point, user quote, or testimonial. Grounds the vision in reality.
6. **The Vision** (5-8s) — Zoom out. What does the world look like with this product in it?
7. **The Ask** (3-5s) — Clear call to action. What do you want the viewer to do?

### Scene-Writing Rules
- **Show, don't tell**: "A parent opens the fridge and sees a glowing alert" > "Our product alerts you"
- **Specific over generic**: "Sarah checks her phone at 6pm and sees 'Chicken expires tomorrow — try this stir fry'" > "Users get notifications"
- **One message per scene**: If you're explaining two things, split into two scenes
- **Duration discipline**: Total should be 30-60 seconds. Longer = less impactful.

## Key Messaging Points

### Tagline
3-5 words. Apply these tests:
- **Receptionist test**: Would a non-technical person understand what you do?
- **Opposite test**: Could a competitor say the opposite? (If not, it's too generic)
- **Memory test**: Can someone repeat it after hearing it once?

**Bad**: "Smart solutions for modern living"
**Good**: "Know your fridge" / "Never waste food again" / "Your kitchen, finally smart"

### Three Value Pillars
One sentence each. Structure: [Benefit] + [How] + [Proof point]

```
1. [Primary benefit] — [How the product delivers it], backed by [evidence].
2. [Secondary benefit] — [How], which [result].
3. [Differentiator] — Unlike [competitor/status quo], [what's different].
```

### Objection Handlers
From Stage 1 debate — the skeptic's top concerns, pre-answered:

```markdown
| Objection | Response | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| "[Skeptic's concern]" | "[Specific counter]" | [From debate round N] |
```

## Stakeholder Rehearsal Integration

When combined with the Stage 3 stakeholder persona:

### Pitch Tailoring
1. **Reorder the elevator pitch** based on stakeholder's decision style:
   - Data-driven → Lead with PROOF, then solution
   - Vision-driven → Lead with HOOK, paint the picture
   - Risk-averse → Lead with ASK (small), show reversibility
   
2. **Adjust storyboard emphasis**:
   - If stakeholder cares about revenue → emphasize Scene 5 (Proof) with market size
   - If stakeholder cares about innovation → emphasize Scene 4 (Magic)
   - If stakeholder cares about risk → add a Scene 6b showing the rollback/pilot plan

3. **Prepare for their specific questions**:
   ```
   STAKEHOLDER: "[Likely question based on persona]"
   COACHED RESPONSE: "[Specific answer using their language and priorities]"
   ```

## Readiness Checklist

Before presenting:
- [ ] Elevator pitch is under 75 words
- [ ] Every claim has evidence from Stage 1
- [ ] Storyboard is under 60 seconds total
- [ ] Tagline passes all three tests
- [ ] Objection handlers cover the top 3 debate concerns
- [ ] Pitch is tailored to the stakeholder's decision style (if Stage 3 was completed)
- [ ] You've rehearsed the stakeholder simulation at least once
