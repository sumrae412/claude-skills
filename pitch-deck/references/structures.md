# Deck Structures

Three canonical structures. Pick one, then deviate consciously.

## Investor Deck (10–12 slides)

Classic flow for raising venture capital, angel funding, or accredited investors.

| # | Slide | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Title** | Company name + one-line description + tagline |
| 2 | **Problem** | The pain you solve, validated with concrete examples |
| 3 | **Solution** | How you solve it — the core insight |
| 4 | **Market** | TAM / SAM / SOM with reasoning |
| 5 | **Product** | What it actually does (screenshot or live demo URL) |
| 6 | **Business Model** | How you make money + unit economics |
| 7 | **Traction** | Evidence of product-market fit (users, revenue, retention) |
| 8 | **Go-to-Market** | How you reach customers + channel economics |
| 9 | **Competition** | Positioning vs alternatives + your moat |
| 10 | **Team** | Why this team will execute |
| 11 | **Financials** | Projections + key metrics + assumptions |
| 12 | **Ask** | How much, for what, milestones it buys |

**Variations:**

- *Seed stage*: lighter on financials, heavier on founder-market fit and early
  traction. The "Ask" slide carries more weight than the financials slide.
- *Series A+*: heavier on retention curves, cohort economics, scaling thesis.
  Traction slide should carry net dollar retention or comparable cohort signal.
- *Cold deck (sent without meeting)*: add an appendix slide with FAQs and a
  one-line context for each non-obvious chart.
- *Demo day*: cap at 10 slides. Cut Competition into Solution (2 lines) and
  cut GTM detail. The ask is implicit (intro requests).

## Sales Deck (8–10 slides)

For B2B sales meetings, enterprise pitches, or warm outbound. Discovery-first.

| # | Slide | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Title** | Your name + their company name |
| 2 | **Their problem** | Stated in their language, not yours |
| 3 | **Cost of inaction** | What it costs them to keep doing nothing |
| 4 | **Solution** | Tailored to their specific situation |
| 5 | **How it works** | Demo, diagram, or workflow |
| 6 | **Proof** | Case studies / testimonials / outcomes from similar customers |
| 7 | **Pricing** | Packages with clear value-to-price mapping |
| 8 | **Next steps** | Specific, scheduled, with named owners |

**Variations:**

- *C-suite*: lead with strategic impact and ROI. Slide 5 ("How it works")
  becomes "Implementation timeline + resourcing."
- *Technical buyer*: slide 5 becomes architecture / integration diagram.
  Add a security & compliance slide before pricing.
- *Re-engagement (returning prospect)*: skip Slides 1–3. Open with what's
  new since their last evaluation.
- *Outbound cold deck*: add a slide 0 with one-sentence "why I'm reaching
  out to you specifically" — keeps it from feeling templated.

## Product-Launch Deck (8–12 slides)

Internal or external launch announcement.

| # | Slide | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Title** | Product name + launch date |
| 2 | **Why now** | Market timing — what made this buildable today |
| 3 | **Problem** | Customer pain we're solving |
| 4 | **Product** | What we built |
| 5 | **Benefits** | What customers can now do that they couldn't before |
| 6 | **Competitive position** | Where we sit vs alternatives |
| 7 | **GTM strategy** | How we'll reach customers (external) or how launch will run (internal) |
| 8 | **Success metrics** | KPIs we'll track post-launch |
| 9 | **Timeline / availability** | When customers can buy / when team should expect work |
| 10 | **Ask** | Press contact, beta list, or internal resource request |

**Variations:**

- *Press / media*: lead with the news angle (slide 1 or 2). Push GTM detail
  to appendix. Include a one-line embargo / availability summary.
- *Internal all-hands*: heavier on GTM execution and cross-team needs.
  "Ask" becomes specific resource requests (engineering support, CS staffing,
  marketing co-op).
- *Beta / design partners*: emphasize what's new since their last build,
  what you need from them (feedback cadence, case-study willingness), and
  roadmap visibility for what's still coming.

## Picking a slide count

Default to the lower bound. Add slides only when each new slide adds a
takeaway the audience needs. Investor decks creep to 20+ slides because
founders keep adding "supporting context" — that context belongs in
appendix, not the main flow.

| Audience time | Recommended slide count |
|---|---|
| 5 min (demo day) | 7–8 main + 5 appendix |
| 10 min (1st meeting) | 10–12 main + 5–10 appendix |
| 20 min (deeper dive) | 12–15 main + appendix as needed |
| 30+ min (working session) | Use a doc, not a deck |
