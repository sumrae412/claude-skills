# Audience Tuning

Same source content, three audiences, three different decks. Know who's
in the room before drafting.

## Investor Audiences

### Venture Capital
- Lead with scalability, market size, return potential
- Heavy on financial projections and unit economics
- Founder-market-fit slot is critical
- Competitive moat / defensibility must be explicit
- "Use of funds" must tie to specific milestones

### Angel Investors
- Lead with personal story and founder passion
- Early validation matters more than projections
- Reasonable ask, faster timeline
- Mention opportunities for value-add involvement (intros, advisory)

### Strategic Corporates
- Lead with strategic alignment to the corporate's roadmap
- Moat slide framed as why-acquire-vs-build
- Less emphasis on standalone economics, more on integration potential

### Accelerator Applications
- Founder-market fit > everything (most predictive variable)
- Concrete progress signals (shipped, traction, kill criteria)
- "What's the hardest unknown" slide rewards self-awareness
- No name-dropping as substitute for evidence

## Sales Audiences

### C-Suite
- Strategic business impact first, features last
- ROI and competitive advantage in the headline
- Risk mitigation and compliance as supporting points
- Implementation timeline and resourcing — concrete, not aspirational

### Technical Buyer (CTO, VP Eng, lead architect)
- Integration and architecture detail in main flow, not appendix
- Security and compliance specs as first-class slides
- Performance benchmarks with methodology
- Roadmap and support model

### Procurement / Operations
- Pricing transparency, total cost of ownership
- SLAs and support tiers
- References from similar-size accounts

### End-User Decision Maker (line-of-business owner)
- Time-to-value as the headline
- Workflow / "day in the life" diagram
- Customer success contacts and case studies from peers
- Implementation effort honest, not minimized

## Launch Audiences

### Press / Media
- Newsworthy angle in the headline of slide 1
- Visual storytelling and demo screenshots
- Customer benefit framing, not feature framing
- Press kit link / embargo terms in appendix

### Internal All-Hands
- GTM execution detail (who does what, when)
- Cross-team asks (engineering support, customer success staffing)
- Success metrics and how each team contributes

### Beta / Design Partners
- What's new since their last build
- What you need from them (feedback cadence, case-study willingness)
- Roadmap visibility — what's still coming

## Stage-Specific Tuning

### Pre-revenue / pre-launch
- "Why now" carries the timing thesis
- Validation evidence: interviews, waitlist, LOIs, design partners
- Kill criteria + validation gates demonstrate operating discipline
- Don't fake traction — name the gap honestly

### Early traction (post-launch, pre-PMF)
- Cohort retention curves > total user count
- Unit economics with actual numbers, not projections
- "What we've learned" slide is high-leverage at this stage

### Post-PMF / scaling
- Net dollar retention, payback period, magic number
- Channel-by-channel CAC and LTV
- Scaling thesis: what gets unlocked with capital

## Register and tone

Match the audience's verbal register:

| Audience | Register |
|---|---|
| VC partner meeting | Direct, data-led, low jargon |
| Angel coffee chat | Personal, story-led, relational |
| Demo day | Energetic, punchy, memorable |
| Enterprise sales | Formal, ROI-led, risk-aware |
| Press | Newsworthy, quotable, vivid |
| Internal all-hands | Candid, execution-focused, accountable |
| Accelerator app | Self-aware, evidence-led, no fluff |
