# Difficult Conversation Preparation Template

Use this worksheet before any challenging workplace conversation.

---

## The Quick Prep (10 minutes)

Use when you don't have much time but still need to prepare.

```markdown
## Quick Conversation Prep

**Topic:** [What is this conversation about?]
**Date/Time Planned:** [When will you have it?]
**With:** [Who?]

---

### The Situation (Facts Only)
[What happened? Observable behavior, no judgment]

### The Impact
[How did this affect you, the team, or the work?]

### My Goal
[What specific outcome do I want?]

### Their Perspective
[How might they see this differently?]

### My Opening Line
[Write the first thing you'll say]
```

## Preparation by Conversation Type

### Performance Conversations

Additional questions to answer before the conversation:

1. What specific examples do I have? (Need at least 2-3)
2. What are the clear expectations they weren't meeting?
3. What support have I provided?
4. What documentation exists?
5. What are the consequences if this continues?
6. What resources can I offer for improvement?

### Conflict Conversations

1. What is the history of this conflict?
2. Have we discussed this before? What happened?
3. Are others involved or affected?
4. Is there a pattern, or is this a one-time incident?
5. What do we have in common that we can build on?
6. What would resolution look like from their side?

### Upward Conversations (Feedback to Manager)

1. How does this align with their priorities?
2. What's in it for them to address this?
3. What political considerations exist?
4. What alternatives can I propose if my first ask is declined?
5. What's my BATNA (best alternative if the conversation doesn't land)?

### Compensation Conversations

1. What is my market value? (Research before going in)
2. What are my key accomplishments since the last adjustment?
3. What is the timing relative to budget cycles?
4. What am I willing to accept besides base salary?
5. What is the company's current financial situation?

## Mental Preparation

### 5-Minute Pre-Conversation Ritual

1. **Ground** (1 min): Feel your feet on the floor; notice 5 things you can see
2. **Breathe** (2 min): In for 4 counts, hold 4, out for 6 — repeat 4 times
3. **Center** (1 min): Hand on heart; say "I can do this"; remember your positive intention
4. **Release** (1 min): Let go of needing to win; you can only control yourself

### Stakes Reality Check

When anxiety is high:
1. What's the worst that could happen?
2. How likely is that, really?
3. If it did happen, could I survive it?
4. What's most likely to happen?
5. What's the best that could happen?

## Common Preparation Mistakes

**Over-preparing**
- You've scripted every word
- You've anticipated 20 possible responses
- You're more anxious, not less
- You're delaying the conversation to "prepare more"

Fix: Prepare themes, not scripts. Know your opening and key points, then trust yourself.

**Under-preparing**
- You haven't thought about their perspective
- You don't have specific examples
- You don't know what outcome you want

Fix: At minimum, complete the Quick Prep above.

**Preparing to attack**
- Your notes focus only on what they did wrong
- You've built a case
- You've decided the outcome before hearing them

Fix: Add — "What might I be missing? What's their perspective?"

**Preparing to avoid**
- Your opening is full of softeners
- You're planning to hint, not state
- You're hoping they'll bring it up first

Fix: Write a direct opening. Say it out loud before you go in.
