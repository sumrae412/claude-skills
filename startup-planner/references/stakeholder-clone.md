# Stakeholder Clone Framework

Build an AI persona of a specific decision-maker so you can rehearse pitches, anticipate objections, and tailor communication. Based on Hiten Shah's "What Would Morgan Do" workflow.

## Why This Works

Generic pitch advice says "know your audience." This technique makes knowing your audience actionable — you construct a behavioral model of the actual person you're pitching to, then simulate the conversation before it happens. The result: you walk in knowing their likely questions, objections, and what earns a yes.

## Gathering Source Material

The persona quality depends entirely on input quality. Gather as much as possible:

### Tier 1: Direct Documents (Best)
- **Operating manual or "How I Work" doc** — if the person has written one, this is gold
- **Emails/messages from them** — reveals communication style, priorities, pet phrases
- **Feedback they've given** — what they praised, what they pushed back on
- **Articles they've shared** — reveals their thinking and values

### Tier 2: Observed Behavior (Good)
- How they run meetings (structured vs. freeform)
- What they ask first when reviewing proposals (cost? impact? risk? timeline?)
- How they react to bad news (shoot the messenger vs. problem-solve)
- What previous pitches they approved vs. rejected — and why

### Tier 3: Public Information (Okay)
- LinkedIn profile, interviews, conference talks
- Company values/priorities they've publicly championed
- Their boss or board's priorities (what cascades down to them)

## Persona Construction

Build the persona around five dimensions:

### 1. Decision Style
How they evaluate proposals:
- **Data-driven**: Won't decide without numbers. Lead with metrics, TAM, projections.
- **Intuition-led**: Goes with gut on people and vision. Lead with story and conviction.
- **Consensus-seeking**: Wants to know who else supports it. Lead with alignment.
- **Risk-calculating**: Weighs downside first. Lead with risk mitigation and reversibility.

### 2. Communication Preference
How they absorb information:
- **Concise executive summary**: One page max, bullets, bottom-line up front
- **Detailed deep-dive**: Wants to see the work, the analysis, the edge cases
- **Visual/demo-first**: "Show me, don't tell me" — prototype > slides
- **Narrative/story-first**: Wants to feel the problem before seeing the solution

### 3. Priority Stack
What they optimize for (rank order):
- Revenue growth
- Cost reduction
- Customer satisfaction
- Team velocity / shipping speed
- Risk avoidance
- Innovation / market position
- Strategic alignment with company direction

### 4. Instant NO Triggers
Proposals die immediately if they:
- [Exceed budget threshold without pre-approval]
- [Require headcount they don't have]
- [Conflict with current strategic priorities]
- [Come without data/evidence]
- [Surprise them in front of their boss]

### 5. YES Accelerators
Proposals gain momentum when they:
- [Solve a problem they've mentioned repeatedly]
- [Come with a prototype or demo]
- [Show clear ROI with a timeline]
- [Align with something their leadership cares about]
- [Include a rollback plan if it fails]

## Using the Persona for Rehearsal

### Pitch Simulation
1. Present the elevator pitch in full
2. Generate 3-5 questions the stakeholder would likely ask — in their voice
3. For each question, provide:
   - Why they're asking (what underlying concern drives it)
   - A coached response (specific language, not generic advice)
   - What NOT to say (common missteps for this person)

### Objection Handling
For each anticipated objection:
```
STAKEHOLDER: "[Objection in their voice]"
WHY: [The underlying concern]
RESPOND: "[Specific coached response]"
AVOID: "[What would make it worse]"
```

### Readiness Rating
After the rehearsal, rate the pitch:
- **Ready**: Pitch addresses their top priorities, answers likely questions, avoids NO triggers
- **Needs Work**: Gaps in 1-2 areas that would cause hesitation
- **Major Gaps**: Missing something that would trigger an instant NO

## Combining with Personality Frameworks

If you know the stakeholder's personality type (Enneagram, MBTI, DISC):

- **Enneagram 1** (Perfectionist): Show thoroughness, acknowledge imperfections proactively
- **Enneagram 3** (Achiever): Lead with success metrics and how this makes them/the team look good
- **Enneagram 5** (Investigator): Come with data, respect their need to process independently
- **Enneagram 8** (Challenger): Be direct, don't soften the ask, show confidence
- **DISC D** (Dominant): Bottom-line first, options not opinions, respect their time
- **DISC I** (Influential): Energy and enthusiasm matter, paint the vision
- **DISC S** (Steady): Show stability, low disruption, team impact
- **DISC C** (Conscientious): Precision, evidence, logical flow
