# Kawasaki Conception Frameworks

Summary of key frameworks from "The Art of the Start 2.0" for the Conception phase.

## 1. Genesis Questions

Great companies begin by answering simple questions, not by pursuing wealth. Six patterns:

| Question | Trigger | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Therefore, what? | Spotted trend → consequences | "Everyone has smartphones with cameras" → Instagram |
| Isn't this interesting? | Curiosity, accidental discovery | Post-it Notes from failed glue experiment |
| Is there a better way? | Frustration with status quo | Wozniak wanted computers accessible to everyone |
| Why doesn't our company do this? | Employer won't listen | You know the customer need, management ignores it |
| It's possible, so why don't we? | Technology enables it | Motorola invented portable phones when phones were places |
| Where is the market leader weak? | Competitor vulnerability | Leader committed to old model, customers dissatisfied, or stopped innovating |

## 2. Sweet Spot (Venn Diagram)

Three overlapping circles:

- **Expertise**: What you and cofounders can actually build
- **Opportunity**: Existing or potential market (do reality check on size)
- **Passion**: Sustainable motivation (or success causes passion?)

Need at least 2 of 3 to start; can develop the third.

## 3. Make Meaning

Test: "If your startup never existed, the world would be worse off because __________."

Meaning ≠ money, power, prestige, or cool office perks. Meaning = making the world better.

If you make meaning, you'll probably also make money.

## 4. Make Mantra

3-4 word phrase explaining the meaning you're making. NOT a mission statement.

**Characteristics:**
- Brief (3-4 words, easily remembered)
- Positive (outward-focused, what you do for customers)
- Not self-serving ("get rich" is not a mantra)

**Examples:**
- Nike: "Authentic athletic performance"
- Disney: "Fun family entertainment"
- eBay: "Democratize commerce"
- Etsy: "Empower craftspeople"

**Tests:**
- Receptionist test: Can your receptionist explain what you do?
- Opposite test: Does competition say the opposite?

## 5. Business Model

Two questions:
1. Who has your money in their pockets?
2. How are you going to get it into your pocket?

**Model types:**
- **Individualized solution**: Deep dive into customer problems, hand-to-hand combat
- **Multicomponent**: Same product, different settings/prices (Coca-Cola)
- **Market leader**: Most innovative, charge premium (Apple)
- **Valuable component**: Ingredient brand (Intel Inside, Dolby)
- **Switchboard**: Control supply (De Beers)
- **Printer and toner**: Base product + refills/upgrades
- **Freemium**: Free tier, pay for more features/capacity
- **Eyeballs**: Attract viewers, sell advertising
- **Virtual goods**: Near-zero cost digital items
- **Craftsman**: Highest quality, may stay small

**Tips:**
- Target specific niche first
- Describe in ≤10 words
- Copy proven models; don't invent new ones
- Expand the pie, don't just grab share

## 6. Soul Mates (Cofounders)

First follower transforms lone nut into leader.

**Similarities needed:**
- Vision (how startup and market will evolve)
- Size ambitions (empire vs lifestyle)
- Commitment level (10 years? family balance?)

**Differences needed:**
- Expertise (builder + seller at minimum)
- Orientation (detail person + big picture person)
- Perspective (diverse backgrounds)

**Warnings:**
- Don't rush (like picking a spouse)
- Don't add cofounders just for fundability
- Assume the best, plan for the worst (everyone vests)

## 7. MATT (Milestones, Assumptions, Tests, Tasks)

### Milestones (most important)
1. Working prototype
2. Initial capital
3. Field-testable version
4. Paying customer
5. Cash-flow breakeven

Spend 80% of effort on these.

### Assumptions (document and test)
- Market size
- Gross margin
- Sales calls per salesperson
- Customer acquisition cost
- Conversion rate
- Sales cycle length
- ROI for customer
- Support calls per unit
- Payment cycles

### Tests (validate assumptions)
- Does acquisition cost permit profit?
- Will people use it?
- Can you afford support?
- Can product handle real-world use?

### Tasks (essential operations)
- Recruit employees
- Find vendors
- Set up accounting/payroll
- File legal documents

Everything not contributing to milestones is low priority.

## Additional Concepts

### DICEE (Product Quality)
- **Deep**: Features customers don't realize they need yet
- **Intelligent**: Shows you understood their pain
- **Complete**: Includes support, docs, ecosystem
- **Empowering**: Makes people better at their work
- **Elegant**: Power + simplicity, what's not there matters

### Positioning
One message, avoid jargon, pass the opposite test, cascade internally, focus on social media not advertising, flow with what market tells you.

### EQ Test (Adviser Vetting)
Questions to separate contenders from pretenders:
- What corporation type? (C corp if Google-esque goals)
- What state to incorporate? (Delaware)
- Do investors need to be accredited? (Yes)
- How should founders split equity? (Not 50/50; reserve for employees and investors)
- Should consultants get options? (No)
- How long should business plan be? (Don't write one; get customers)
