# Discovery Interview — Branching Questions

When Phase 2 surfaces gaps, run a structured interview to uncover experience the user didn't put on their resume. Every question targets a specific gap; every answer is evaluated for substance before becoming a bullet.

## Entry Criteria

Run Phase 3 if:

- 3+ bullets in `GAP`/`WEAK` bands, OR
- Any must-have keyword has no `DIRECT` or `TRANSFERABLE` coverage, OR
- User explicitly asks "what else should I include?"

Skip if all must-haves are covered at `TRANSFERABLE`+ and the user has not asked.

## Ground Rules

- **One gap at a time.** Don't paste 10 questions at once.
- **Ask for specifics, not generics.** "Tell me about a time you X" beats "Do you have X experience?"
- **Probe for quantifiables.** Team size, duration, outcome, before/after, who else was involved.
- **Listen for disqualifiers.** If the user hedges ("kind of", "not really the lead"), reflect that back — don't paper over it.
- **Never invent.** If the user can't substantiate, mark the gap as real and route to positioning / cover letter.

## Branching Taxonomy

Match each gap to one of these branches. The branch chooses the opening question.

### A. Skill gap (technical or functional)

*"The JD calls for `[X]`. Have you done `[X]` in any context — side project, internship, prior role I don't see listed, informal help on another team's project?"*

Follow-ups:

- "What did you actually build or do?"
- "Who used it, or who depended on it?"
- "How long was the project?"

If positive: candidate bullet at `TRANSFERABLE` or `ADJACENT` band (rarely `DIRECT` for undocumented work).

### B. Scope gap (seniority signals)

*"The JD implies `[scope, e.g., managing 10+ people / $1M budget / multi-team coordination]`. Walk me through the largest scope you've owned, even if your title didn't reflect it."*

Follow-ups:

- "Who reported to you formally vs. informally?"
- "What was the blast radius if you made a bad call?"
- "What did you measure or watch to know you owned it?"

Listen for dotted-line leadership and project-scale ownership that wasn't in the title.

### C. Industry gap

*"The JD is in `[industry]`. What's the closest work you've done in a similar context — same user type, same business model, same regulatory environment, adjacent domain?"*

Follow-ups:

- "What translates? What doesn't?"
- "Have you worked with `[industry]`-adjacent customers or partners?"

Use Abstraction Level reframing (zoom out to the common capability).

### D. Quantification gap

*"Your bullet says '`[qualitative description]`'. What were the numbers? Rough estimates are fine — team size, duration, user count, revenue impact, % improvement."*

Follow-ups:

- "What's a conservative estimate? What's the aggressive one?"
- "How did you know it moved? What did you measure?"

Round to single-significant-figure estimates if the user is uncertain. Never fabricate.

### E. Recency gap (stale skill)

*"The JD lists `[X]` and the last time I see it on your resume is `[year]`. Have you kept up — side projects, evaluations, work-adjacent exposure?"*

Follow-ups:

- "How confident would you be jumping back in on day one?"
- "Anything formal — course, cert, recent reading?"

If genuinely stale, recommend honesty: cover it in the cover letter as a re-up plan, not in the resume.

## Candidate Bullet Output

For each productive answer, draft:

```
GAP SOURCE:       "Product analytics at scale" (must-have keyword, unaddressed in Phase 2)
BRANCH:           A (Skill gap)
USER ANSWER:      Built Looker dashboards at prior role, ~200 weekly viewers, ran monthly business reviews
CANDIDATE BULLET: "Built and maintained executive analytics dashboards (Looker) with ~200 weekly viewers, anchoring monthly business reviews for a 40-person org."
PROPOSED BAND:    TRANSFERABLE 70%
PLACEMENT:        Prior role (2020–2022), as new bullet 3
ACCURATE?         [ask user]
```

## When to Stop

- User visibly tired or disengaging → pause, summarize what's been added, offer to continue later.
- Answer would require fabrication → stop, mark as real gap, route to positioning.
- Diminishing returns (last 2 questions yielded nothing new) → wrap up.

## Gap Disclosure vs. Gap Filling

A real gap — something the user hasn't done — belongs in the cover letter or the interview, framed as a re-up plan or an adjacency they're explicit about. **It does not belong in the resume as manufactured content.** This is the truth-preserving principle applied to discovery.
