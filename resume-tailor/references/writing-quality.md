# Writing Quality

Load this reference before drafting any summary, headline set, or cover letter. The skill is allowed to be structured in its analysis; the final writing must not read like analysis.

## 1. Core Standard

The writing should sound authored, not synthesized.

That means:

- one live idea per sentence
- concrete nouns and verbs
- specific constraints, stakes, or outcomes
- varied sentence length
- no stack of interchangeable buzzwords

If a sentence could fit any candidate or any company, rewrite it.

## 2. What "AI slop" Looks Like

Reject sentences with these patterns:

- stacked abstractions: "strategy, execution, and innovation"
- hollow praise: "exciting opportunity", "dynamic company", "strong alignment"
- generic intensity: "deeply", "truly", "incredibly", "highly"
- mirrored list cadence across paragraphs
- thesisless paragraphing where each sentence is just another claim
- identity inflation: "uniquely positioned", "rare blend", "exceptional ability"

## 3. Stronger Replacements

Prefer:

- named problems over job-description paraphrase
- concrete evidence over self-description
- one sharp differentiator over three vague ones
- verbs that imply action under constraint: built, untangled, shipped, stabilized, translated, reduced, rebuilt

Examples:

- Weak: "I bring a strong combination of technical depth, strategic thinking, and leadership."
- Better: "I build production AI systems, explain them to decision-makers, and leave behind workflows other teams can keep using."

- Weak: "Your mission and the role strongly align with my background."
- Better: "This role sits at the intersection of production AI, stakeholder translation, and durable systems work, which is where I've spent the last several years."

- Weak: "At DeepLearning.AI, I created curriculum and educational content on AI topics."
- Better: "At DeepLearning.AI, I turned fast-moving LLM and agentic-system patterns into tooling, frameworks, and training that helped builders adopt them faster."

- Weak: "I moved into education to share my experience with others."
- Better: "I moved closer to where new AI patterns were emerging so I could translate them into repeatable practices other teams could ship with."

- Weak: "Python, TensorFlow, SQL, AWS, LLMs, RAG, agents, leadership."
- Better: "AI roadmap ownership, agentic-system strategy, regulated production governance, and executive stakeholder alignment, with Python and model-stack fluency as working knowledge."

## 4. Paragraph Test

Every paragraph needs:

1. A governing idea in the first sentence.
2. One concrete detail that proves it.
3. A clean landing sentence or clause.

If a paragraph is just three adjacent claims, it will read like a list even when it is written in sentences.

## 5. Read-Aloud Test

Before sign-off, read the draft aloud and cut anything that sounds like:

- a slogan
- a LinkedIn post
- a consultant bio
- a paragraph assembled from resume bullets

If the reader can predict the ending of the sentence after the first five words, the sentence is probably too generic.
