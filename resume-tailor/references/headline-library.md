# Headline Library

Load this file before drafting headline options in Phase 4. Use it as a pattern library, not a phrasebook to copy blindly.

## 1. Headline Standard

A strong headline should do three things:

- name the candidate's lane
- imply the level of work
- distinguish the candidate without turning into branding fluff

If the headline could sit on a LinkedIn influencer banner, it is too vague.

## 2. Patterns by Target

### Executive / Head / VP

- "AI leader turning regulated, high-stakes systems into production capability"
- "Head of AI focused on roadmap, governance, and enterprise adoption"
- "AI executive translating frontier models into durable production systems"

Use when the role is about direction, operating model, and organizational leverage.

### Strategic technical leader

- "Product and AI leader building production systems teams can actually ship"
- "AI product leader focused on LLM adoption, evaluation, and operational scale"
- "Technical leader connecting model capability to business deployment"

Use when the role mixes strategy and credible technical depth.

### Builder-operator

- "AI builder-operator shipping production systems under real constraints"
- "Hands-on AI leader turning prototypes into reliable workflows"
- "Technical operator focused on production AI, reuse, and adoption"

Use when the target company still wants visible hands-on signal.

### DeepLearning.AI / field-shaping transition

- "AI product leader translating emerging LLM patterns into reusable systems"
- "Leader bridging frontier AI workflows and real-world adoption"
- "Operator focused on scaling how AI gets built, not just what gets built"

Use when the current role could be misread as education-only work.

## 3. Anti-Patterns

Reject headlines that:

- stack nouns with no point of view
- lead with generic identity words like "innovative" or "dynamic"
- repeat the job title without adding meaning
- promise broad excellence instead of naming a real lane

Weak:

- "Innovative AI executive driving transformation"
- "Strategic leader with technical depth and business acumen"
- "AI/ML | GenAI | LLM | RAG | Agents"

Better:

- "AI executive focused on roadmap, governance, and production adoption"
- "Leader translating production AI complexity into usable operating systems"

## 4. Final Test

For each headline, ask:

1. Could another senior AI candidate use this unchanged?
2. Does it name a real lane rather than a bag of traits?
3. Does it set up the summary and bullets, or fight them?

If the answer to 1 is yes, rewrite it.
