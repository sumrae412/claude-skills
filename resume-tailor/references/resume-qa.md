# Resume QA

Load this file before the final section-by-section review in Phase 5. This is the last pass for prose quality and document coherence.

## 1. Section-Level Checks

- Does the headline set up the story the resume actually tells?
- If there is a summary, does it add anything the first role does not already prove?
- Does `# SKILLS` match the target level, or does it down-level the candidate?

## 2. Role-Level Checks

For each role:

- Does the first bullet earn its spot?
- Are two bullets making the same point with different wording?
- Is there at least one bullet showing result, leverage, or decision quality?
- For executive roles, is there visible judgment rather than just delivery?

## 3. Document-Level Checks

- Does the resume have a governing idea, or does it read like a warehouse of facts?
- Does the recent experience feel at least as strong as the older experience?
- Are pivots explained rather than left for the reader to guess?
- Does the document sound like one person with one point of view?

## 4. Red Flags

Fix before export if any of these are true:

- the summary is weaker than the first bullet
- the skills section is more specific than the experience section
- the strongest achievement is buried in the middle of a role
- the document keeps repeating the same capability in slightly different language
- the writing alternates between executive language and IC language without intent

## 5. Final Read

Read the resume top to bottom and ask:

1. What is the single strongest impression this leaves?
2. Is that the impression the target role needs?
3. What is the weakest sentence in the document?

Delete or rewrite the weakest sentence before final export.
