# Positioning — Narrative, Level, Gap Handling

Phase 4 of the tailoring flow — always runs. Positioning is about how the resume reads as a *story*, not just a keyword match. Four dimensions: headline/summary, narrative arc, level calibration, gap handling.

## Headline + Summary

Offer 2–3 headline options at different angles. Show the framing choice so the user can pick intentionally.

- **Function-forward:** "Product Manager specializing in 0→1 B2B SaaS"
- **Outcome-forward:** "Product leader who has shipped 4 products to $1M+ ARR"
- **Angle-forward:** "PM/eng hybrid — ships product fast and unblocks the team"

Summary (2–3 sentences): optional for PMs and execs, often skippable for engineers. Include only if it adds a dimension the bullets can't — e.g., cross-functional trajectory, industry focus, operating mode.

## Narrative Arc

Read the resume top to bottom and tell the user what story it tells. Then name what story the JD wants to hear. Identify the delta.

- **Ascent:** each role larger scope than the last. Strongest arc for promotion applications.
- **Specialization:** progressive depth in one domain. Strong for IC / staff+ roles.
- **Pivot:** clean change in function or industry. Needs to be explained in summary, not hidden.
- **Breadth:** multiple functions or industries. Positions as adaptability, but only if the JD rewards it.
- **Founder/operator:** mix of scale and 0→1. Lean into the specific mix the JD wants.

**Common arc problem:** strong early career, weaker recent. Fix — lead with outcomes not titles; re-emphasize the recent role's impact with quantifiables from Phase 2's matching pass.

## Level Calibration

Check the resume's language register against target seniority.

| Register signal | Junior | Mid | Senior | Staff+/Exec |
|-----------------|--------|-----|--------|-------------|
| Verbs | "contributed", "helped", "learned" | "shipped", "owned", "led" | "drove", "scaled", "architected" | "set direction", "aligned orgs", "defined strategy" |
| Scope | single tasks | features | projects / products | functions / orgs |
| Outcomes | completion | delivery | impact metrics | business outcomes |
| Failure framing | absent | "challenges" | "trade-offs" | "bets" |

Calibration output:

- Where is the resume currently pitched?
- Where should it be pitched for this JD?
- List 3–5 specific phrases to adjust, with before/after.

**Don't over-correct upward.** A resume pitched two levels above actual experience fails the interview. The goal is accurate match, not inflation.

## Gap Handling

Visible gaps — career pauses, short tenures, industry changes, laid-off clusters, unemployment gaps. Every gap needs a *disclosure choice*:

1. **Address on the resume** (brief, factual)
   - Career break: "Career break, 2022–2023 — family caregiving" as a one-liner in the timeline.
   - Layoff: no resume treatment; the dates speak for themselves.
2. **Defer to cover letter** (contextualize)
   - Industry pivot: cover letter explains the "why now"; resume demonstrates the "how".
   - Short tenure: cover letter owns it; resume doesn't explain.
3. **Defer to interview** (don't preemptively address)
   - Normal 1–2 month gaps between roles are too minor to flag.

**Rule of thumb:** if a gap is *both* visible *and* unusual enough to generate questions in screening, address it briefly on the resume. Everything else → cover letter or interview.

## Positioning Checkpoint

End Phase 4 with:

```
HEADLINE OPTIONS (pick one):
  A. [function-forward]   "Product Manager specializing in 0→1 B2B SaaS"
  B. [outcome-forward]    "Product leader who has shipped 4 products to $1M+ ARR"
  C. [angle-forward]      "PM/eng hybrid — ships product fast and unblocks the team"

NARRATIVE ARC:
  Current: [what the resume currently tells]
  Target:  [what the JD wants to hear]
  Delta:   [the gap and how we're closing it]

LEVEL CALIBRATION:
  Current pitch: [level]
  Target pitch:  [level]
  Adjustments:   [list of before/after phrase changes]

GAP HANDLING:
  Visible gaps:    [list]
  Disclosure plan: [per gap: resume / cover letter / interview]

Ask: "Which headline? Any narrative or level adjustments you want me to push harder or pull back?"
```

## When Positioning Drifts Beyond Resume

If the user starts asking about career direction, longer-term strategy, or whether to apply at all — stop and name the boundary. That's career-coaching territory, not resume tailoring. Suggest a licensed career coach.

If the conversation surfaces acute distress (burnout, imposter spirals, job-loss anxiety driving compulsive rewriting) — pause. Resume decisions made under acute distress often need to be unwound later. Suggest talking to a therapist or EAP before continuing. The resume can wait.
