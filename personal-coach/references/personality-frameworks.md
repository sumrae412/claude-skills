# Personality Frameworks Reference

Summaries of each framework and how to apply them in coaching. Use these to interpret user profiles and tailor coaching responses.

---

## Enneagram

9 personality types based on core motivations, fears, and defense mechanisms.

| Type | Core Motivation | Stress Direction | Growth Direction |
|------|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1 - Reformer | Be right, improve | → 4 (moody) | → 7 (spontaneous) |
| 2 - Helper | Be needed, loved | → 8 (aggressive) | → 4 (self-aware) |
| 3 - Achiever | Succeed, be admired | → 9 (disengaged) | → 6 (loyal) |
| 4 - Individualist | Be unique, authentic | → 2 (clingy) | → 1 (principled) |
| 5 - Investigator | Be competent, conserve | → 7 (scattered) | → 8 (decisive) |
| 6 - Loyalist | Be secure, supported | → 3 (image-focused) | → 9 (calm) |
| 7 - Enthusiast | Be happy, avoid pain | → 1 (critical) | → 5 (focused) |
| 8 - Challenger | Be strong, in control | → 5 (withdrawn) | → 2 (caring) |
| 9 - Peacemaker | Maintain peace, avoid conflict | → 6 (anxious) | → 3 (assertive) |

**Coaching angles:** Wings modify core type. Under stress, people move to their stress direction (unhealthy traits of that type). Growth = consciously adopting healthy traits of the growth direction.

## Myers-Briggs (MBTI)

4 dichotomies producing 16 types. Focus on communication and decision-making style.

**Dichotomies:**
- **E/I** (Extraversion/Introversion) — Energy source: external interaction vs. internal reflection
- **S/N** (Sensing/Intuition) — Information: concrete facts vs. patterns and possibilities
- **T/F** (Thinking/Feeling) — Decisions: logic and consistency vs. values and harmony
- **J/P** (Judging/Perceiving) — Structure: planned and decisive vs. flexible and spontaneous

**Coaching implications:**
- E types process by talking; I types need time to reflect before responding
- S types want specific examples; N types prefer big-picture framing
- T types respond to logical arguments; F types need values alignment
- J types want clear action plans; P types want options and flexibility

## DISC

4 behavioral styles focused on workplace interaction patterns.

| Style | Focus | Pace | Under Pressure |
|-------|-------|------|----------------|
| **D** (Dominance) | Results, control | Fast, decisive | Becomes blunt, impatient |
| **I** (Influence) | People, enthusiasm | Fast, spontaneous | Becomes disorganized, emotional |
| **S** (Steadiness) | Stability, support | Methodical, patient | Becomes passive, resistant to change |
| **C** (Conscientiousness) | Accuracy, quality | Careful, analytical | Becomes overly critical, withdrawn |

**Coaching implications:**
- D: Give bottom-line answers, let them lead. Don't over-explain.
- I: Be enthusiastic, acknowledge their ideas. Don't be too formal.
- S: Be patient, provide reassurance. Don't rush decisions.
- C: Provide data and logic. Don't be vague or emotional.

## Human Design

Combines astrology, I Ching, Kabbalah, and chakra system. Focus on decision-making strategy and energy type.

**Types:**
| Type | Population | Strategy | Signature Feeling |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Manifestor | ~9% | Inform before acting | Peace |
| Generator | ~37% | Wait to respond | Satisfaction |
| Manifesting Generator | ~33% | Wait to respond, then inform | Satisfaction |
| Projector | ~20% | Wait for invitation | Success |
| Reflector | ~1% | Wait a lunar cycle | Surprise |

**Authority** (decision-making center): Emotional, Sacral, Splenic, Ego, Self-Projected, Mental, Lunar

**Coaching implications:**
- Generators/MGs: Ask yes/no questions to activate sacral response
- Projectors: Don't push; guide and wait for recognition
- Manifestors: Support their initiative; help them communicate impact
- Authority type determines HOW to coach decision-making

### MBTI
- Type: [e.g., INFJ]

### DISC

- Secondary style: [if known]

### Human Design
- Type: [e.g., Projector]
- Authority: [e.g., Emotional]
- Profile: [e.g., 4/6]
- Strategy: [e.g., Wait for invitation]

### Additional Context
- Values I prioritize:
- Communication preferences:
- Known stress triggers:
- Growth areas I'm working on:
