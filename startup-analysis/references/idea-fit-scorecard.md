# Idea Fit Scorecard

Source: Summer's "Idea Fit Scorecard (Sharable)" Google Sheet
(https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1ksPk82Nbj39TH29MDbJA8-sexTvOAzxBvjiCD9-YjbM),
imported 2026-07-12.

## What this is for

Phase 7's 6-axis scorecard (`phase-7-verdict.md`) scores whether the
**market** wants the idea — pain, buyer clarity, urgency, differentiation,
speed to validate, founder-market fit as a single line item.

This scorecard scores whether **this idea fits this founder** —
weekly-hours reality, funding model, geography, ambition ceiling, and
whether the money and execution difficulty pencil out against the
founder's own targets. It is a personal-fit filter, not a market filter.
Run both — a market-validated idea can still be a bad personal fit (wrong
pace, wrong funding model, ceiling below ambition), and this scorecard is
what catches that.

Use it standalone for a quick gut-check, or run it inside this skill
after Phase 4 (Competitive Wedge) once ICP/wedge/GTM give enough detail
to estimate the Economic Fit inputs — see "Where this plugs into
CLEARFRAME" below.

## Step 1 — Founder profile (fill once, reused across every idea scored)

Ask the founder directly; do not estimate these — they are preferences,
not facts to infer.

**The life you want:**
- Weekly hours willing to commit
- Funding model wanted (bootstrap / raise / hybrid)
- Growth pace wanted (slow-and-steady / fast-and-aggressive)
- Geography (remote-friendly / ties you to a place)
- Team willing to run (solo / small / large)

**Ambition:**
- Outcome wanted (cashflow business you keep / exit)
- Scale aiming at
- Target annual revenue ($)

**The fork:**
- Willing to bend your life to the idea, or does the idea have to bend
  to your life?

## Step 2 — Score the idea against that profile

Three fit categories, weighted: **Founder fit 40%, Economic fit 40%,
Execution fit 20%.** Score each line item; cite the evidence or mark it
an estimate — no invented numbers presented as facts (same guardrail as
the rest of this skill).

### Founder fit — what the idea demands (vs. the profile above)

| Line item | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Weekly hours the idea realistically demands | vs. hours willing to commit — if it demands more, founder fit drops |
| Funding it needs | bootstrappable from revenue, or does it need outside capital — vs. funding model wanted |
| Pace it forces | fast/aggressive vs. slow-build — vs. pace wanted |
| Geography it requires | remote-run vs. ties you to a place — vs. geography preference |
| Team it needs | solo/small vs. big team — vs. team willing to run |
| Outcome it leads to | cashflow-you-keep vs. exit — vs. outcome wanted |
| Its realistic ceiling | size this could realistically reach — vs. scale you're aiming for; too small for your ambition scores low |
| How much you want to work on this (1–5, 5=desperately) | genuine desire for *this specific thing*, not just the money |
| Willing to commit 5+ years? (1–5, 5=hell yes) | long-haul stick test |

### Economic fit — does the money work? (target: founder's target annual revenue)

| Line item | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Number of customers in the market | realistic addressable count |
| Annual value per customer ($) | price or annual spend per customer |
| Realistic share you'd win — low (%) | conservative market-share capture |
| Realistic share you'd win — high (%) | optimistic market-share capture |
| → Attainable revenue — LOW *(calculated)* | customers × value × low share |
| → Attainable revenue — HIGH *(calculated)* | customers × value × high share |
| → vs. target *(calculated)* | does the attainable range clear the founder's target revenue? |
| How much are customers spending on this today? (1–5, 5=high) | existing willingness to pay |
| Is it proven, or do you have to prove it? (1–5, 5=proven) | demand-proof burden |

### Execution fit — the way

| Line item | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Idea difficulty (1–5, 5=hard) | harder = less competition but riskier |
| Time to a working prototype | shorter = faster to real signal |
| Your ability to execute this specific idea (1–5, 5=strong) | scored against the difficulty line above |
| Current experience in this market (1–5, 5=experienced) | how much you already know this market |

## Step 3 — Roll up

- Overall fit score = weighted blend (Founder 40 / Economic 40 /
  Execution 20).
- Verdict = plain-English read of the overall score.
- Report the three category sub-scores alongside the overall — a high
  blended score hiding one collapsed category (e.g. Economic fit failing
  the target-revenue check) is exactly the kind of false-positive this
  scorecard exists to catch.

## Where this plugs into CLEARFRAME

Run Step 1 once per founder (not per idea). Run Step 2/3 after Phase 4
(Competitive Wedge) — Economic Fit needs a wedge-sized customer count and
realistic share estimate, which Phase 1 (Market Reality) and Phase 4
should already have produced or can produce on request. Feed the
category sub-scores into Phase 7 as an additional evidence column
alongside the 6-axis market scorecard — a Go verdict should not ignore a
collapsed Founder or Economic fit sub-score even if the market-fit axes
are strong.

## Guardrails

- Founder-profile fields (Step 1) are preferences — ask, never infer.
- Economic Fit numbers are estimates unless the founder supplies real
  data or Phase 1/4 already produced sized figures — label them as
  estimates.
- A collapsed category (any sub-score reading as a clear fail) is a
  structural flag worth surfacing on its own, not just folded into the
  blended total.
