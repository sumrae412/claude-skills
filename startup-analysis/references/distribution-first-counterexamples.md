# Distribution-first ideation — counterexamples to problem-first framing

The CLEARFRAME stack defaults to problem-first / wedge-first reasoning (Phase 1 market reality → Phase 2 ICP → Phase 4 competitive wedge). For some categories — especially consumer B2C — the right framing is reversed: pick a viral, scalable format first; the product is whatever the format requires. Hold this lens as a checked alternative during Phase 4–5 when the idea is consumer-facing.

Key reframings worth surfacing during pressure-testing of B2C ideas:

- **Format over problem:** "Your viral potential determines your ceiling. Pick a viral, scalable, and sustainable format first, then build the minimum app it requires."
- **The moat is distribution:** "Acquirers buy attention they couldn't build themselves."
- **Where to find formats:** Study winning apps (Halo AI prank format, UMax face rating); steal from e-commerce / info products with viral PMF but weak product economics.
- **Retention pairs with format:** "Churn determines your acquisition multiple. Pick a format that funnels into a product people use habitually."
- **Inbound from viral content:** Matt's $375k exit started when Jenni AI reached out after mistaking his TikTok presence for a creator partnership.

## When to apply

- The idea is consumer-facing (B2C app, browser extension, mobile experience)
- The target user discovers products via short-form video / social, not via search / referral / sales
- The expected exit profile is acquisition-by-distribution (not enterprise contract, not subscription scale)

## When to ignore

- B2B / prosumer / vertical SaaS — the default CLEARFRAME flow is correct; problem and wedge dominate
- Regulated industries (health, legal, finance) where format-first triggers compliance failure
- Product categories where retention requires craft, not virality (dev tools, content workflows, vertical operator tools)
