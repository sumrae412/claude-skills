# Hooks, Delivery, and Iteration

Load when sharpening a drafted pitch — opening hooks, spoken delivery, and how
to test and refine across real conversations.

## Villain + hero analysis (do this before writing lines)

Fill these out first; the pitch lines fall out of them.

```
## THE VILLAIN: [problem name]
Victims:    [who suffers]
Damage:     [quantified pain — money lost, time wasted, lives affected]
Disguise:   [why hasn't this been solved? what makes it tricky?]
Weakness:   [the insight that lets you beat it]

## THE HERO: [company name]
Weapon:       [your unique solution / approach]
Origin story: [why you? what makes you the one to win?]
Proof:        [evidence the weapon works]
Quest:        [the world you're creating]
```

## Opening hooks library

Adapt to the specific business; never ship the bracketed template as-is.

**Problem recognition:**
- "[Big number] [companies/people] still [outdated behavior]."
- "You've probably noticed that [observable trend]."
- "The last time [industry] really changed was [date]."

**Urgency / timing:**
- "[Trend] is accelerating — the window to own [market] is [timeframe]."
- "[Recent competitor funding or market-timing event]."

**Credibility:**
- "I spent [X years] at [notable company], where I watched [insight]."
- "[Notable person / company] is already [using / advising / investing]."

**Contrast:**
- "Unlike [alternative], we [key difference]."
- "Everyone else [common approach]. We [contrarian approach]."
- "The old way: [pain]. Our way: [gain]."

Run each candidate hook through the generic-swap test (§9 of the shared
communication principles) — if it works for a competitor, it is not a hook yet.

## Delivery coaching (spoken pitches)

**Body language:** grounded stance, shoulders back. Eye contact during the
villain setup to draw them in. Hands to show scale and transformation. Pause
before the ask — let the tension sit.

**Voice:** lower and slower for the villain (gravity); lift energy for the hero
(excitement); slow down on key numbers so they land; keep momentum on context.

## Mistakes that kill spoken pitches

- Opening with the company name.
- Feature-dumping before the listener hates the villain.
- Vague market sizing ("it's a huge market").
- No clear ask.
- Apologizing or hedging ("we're still early, but...").
- Not knowing when to stop — leave them wanting the meeting.

## Iteration framework

**A/B what you test:**

| Test | Version A | Version B | Measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook | Stat-based | Story-based | Did they lean in? |
| Villain | Industry-level | Personal story | Emotional response |
| Proof | Metrics | Testimonial | Credibility read |
| Ask | Specific | Open-ended | Did they convert? |

**After each real pitch, capture:**
- Did they lean in or lean back?
- What did they ask?
- What did they remember when they summarized it back?
- Did they offer an intro or a follow-up?

**Refinement triggers:**
- Same question from 3+ listeners → add that clarity to the pitch.
- Confusion about the market → strengthen the villain setup.
- "What makes you different?" → sharpen the hero's one differentiator.
- No follow-up → revisit urgency and the ask.
