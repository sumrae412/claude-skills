# Practice and Scoring

Load when a draft pitch exists and the founder wants to pressure-test it before
the real conversation. Two tools: a self-score rubric to find the weak
dimension, and an investor roleplay to stress-test delivery and Q&A.

## Pitch scoring rubric

Score each dimension 1-10. The point is not the total — it is finding the one
or two dimensions dragging the pitch down, then fixing those before anything
else.

| # | Dimension | A 9-10 looks like | A 1-3 looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Clarity** | A non-expert repeats it back correctly (the 2-Sentence Test) | Needs jargon or a diagram to follow |
| 2 | **Lead with strength** | Strongest dimension lands in the first 60s | Best card buried at the end |
| 3 | **Traction honesty** | Metrics paired with timeframes, signups vs active distinguished | Vanity metrics, no timeframe, inflated |
| 4 | **Insight quality** | A non-obvious truth the listener did not already know | Restates the obvious problem |
| 5 | **Market sizing** | Bottom-up (customers x revenue), assumptions stated | "It's a huge market" / top-down only |
| 6 | **Business model** | One sentence on how money is made | Vague or missing |
| 7 | **Team credentials** | Specific verifiable wins, founder-market fit | Titles and bios, no proof |
| 8 | **Ask clarity** | Amount + milestone + timeframe | "We're raising a round" |

Rough verdict: average **8+** is ready to pitch; **6-7** needs one more pass on
the low dimensions; **below 6** is not ready — fix the lowest two first.

## Investor roleplay (practice mode)

Have Claude (or a person) play an investor and stress-test the pitch live.

**How to run it:**

1. Pick a persona from `investor-types.md` (by what they optimize for, or by
   funding stage). State it: "You are a seed VC who optimizes for traction."
2. Deliver the pitch as written.
3. The investor asks 3-5 of their characteristic questions (below), pushes on
   the weakest claim, and tries one real objection.
4. After, give feedback on three axes only: did it **land** (was the value
   clear), **pacing** (too slow / rushed / right), and **answer strength** (did
   the Q&A responses hold up or fold).
5. Fix the weakest answer, then re-run with a different persona.

**Top investor questions to expect (prepare an answer for each):**

- Why now? What changed that makes this possible today?
- Why you and this team specifically?
- How big can this get, and how do you know?
- What stops a bigger company from doing this?
- How do you make money, and what are the unit economics?
- What's your traction, and is it growing?
- What are you raising, and what does it unlock?
- What's the biggest risk you're not telling me about?

**Objection handling:** name the weakness before they do. "The honest gap is X;
here's how we close it." Flagging a real risk reads as credibility; hiding it
reads as naivety or spin.
